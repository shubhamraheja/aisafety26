Over-Flagged Examples

2 Politicians in election coverage
3 Forum usernames such as axboi87, placeholder names like Jane Doe and Bob Smith in a patent document

Root cause: Likely because of limited or ambiguous local context, a limitation also noted by OpenAI. Conservative by design (perfect recall, low precision). Adjusting this behaviour would require fine-tuning. Good for general data sanitation, but needs more work for a large pretraining corpus of wide variety.

Critical Limitations

  • No false negatives in sample. This does not mean the model is perfect because our sample size is very small.
  • No harm detection: Filter only flags PII. Harmful content not caught (2 samples undetected)
  • Annotation subjectivity: Public figure names are borderline; different annotators may differ
Critical gap: PII detection might not be enough for pretraining. The model does not have a flag for harmful content. Black-magic and gambling content passed through the filter undetected. Annotation is also subjective: whether a public figure's name counts as private is a borderline case.

Conclusions & Recommendations

Key Takeaways

  • Data quality low: Mean 4.5/10, half requires cleaning
  • Privacy leaks in PDFs: Institutional documents slip through
  • Harmful content undetected: Separate classifiers needed
  • Automated detection limited: Can't distinguish public figures from private PII
  • Unexpected diversity: Different per-source distributions, multilingual unlabeled

For Better Curation

  • Multi-tier approach: Automated flagging + manual review + dataset policies
  • Separate harm classifiers: Privacy filter only catches PII
  • Language metadata: Flag non-English samples explicitly
  • Source audit: Different sources have different failure modes
Bottom line: Hand-inspection reveals nuances automated tools miss: institutional leakage, harmful content, annotation subjectivity. PII detection alone is not enough for pretraining. Curation requires both automation and human judgment.
