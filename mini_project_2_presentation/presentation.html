Key finding: Human responses vary wildly depending on the prompt's actual needs. Model output is ~2.4× more verbose on average and shows a bounded-narrow length distribution, likely constrained by max_tokens=256.
Why this matters: The model cannot write short answers. Even when a human would respond with a single word, the model produces 30+ words. This consistent verbosity is itself a strong detection signal.

Part 2.1

The "Mammal" Anomaly

Prompt: "Which of the following animals is a mammal: Human, Dog, Elephants, Cat, Bats, Goat, Bears, Sheep, Wolf, Lion, Squirrel, Koala, Cheetah, Deer, Orca, Gorillas, Camel, Hamsters, Hedgehog, Blue whale, Chimpanzee"

Human Response
All of them are mammals! They have very distinct characteristics and live in different environments. However, it's characteristic of mammals that they have three middle ear bones, milk-producing mammary glands, neocortex brain regions, and fur or hair.
~38 words
Qwen3-4B Response
All of the animals listed are mammals.

Let's go through the list:
Human → Mammal ✔  Dog → Mammal ✔  Elephant → Mammal ✔  Cat → Mammal ✔  Bats → Mammal ✔  Goat → Mammal ✔ … (continues for all 21 items)
~150+ words (truncated by max_tokens)
Why "mammal" had ratio 21.0: The human answered concisely in one sentence. The model repeated "Mammal" 21 times in a mechanical checklist format. This single prompt is the entire source of the anomalous unigram ratio.

Part 2.2

Output Quality: Watermarked vs. Plain

Finding: Watermarked and plain outputs are qualitatively indistinguishable in fluency and hallucination rate. The δ bias does not noticeably affect content or coherence.

Example prompt: "What athlete created the 'Beast Quake' for the Seattle Seahawks?"

Plain Output
The "Beast Quake" refers to a powerful, violent earthquake that occurred in Seattle on April 29, 1962. This is not related to an athlete or the Seattle Seahawks, as the Seahawks were founded in 1976.
Watermarked Output
The "Beast Quake" is a term used to describe the powerful, intense crowd noise that fans of the Seattle Seahawks create during home games at Qwest Field (now Lumen Field).
Note: Both outputs hallucinate (neither correctly identifies Marshawn Lynch). The watermark does not make hallucinations worse or better. Quality degradation from the watermark is negligible.
